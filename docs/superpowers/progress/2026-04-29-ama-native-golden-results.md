## Why not 100%?

The original spec (Section 8.4) called for x86_64 native to match wasm 1:1
because both go through SSE intrinsics. That assumption was wrong.

Concretely, the two builds diverge because:

- **emscripten emulation layer** maps `_mm_*` → `v128.*` wasm opcodes via its
  emul-SSE header. The lowered semantics are *equivalent* but not
  bit-identical to native x86 SSE in every case.
- **compiler optimization** differs: emcc (LLVM-based) and Apple Clang have
  separate cost models, FMA/contraction policies, and instruction selection.
- ama's beam search **breaks tied scores** by iteration order; that order
  depends on small variations in the `Field` representation's internal layout
  which in turn depends on compiler-generated code paths.

The result: in ~7.5% of positions, multiple candidates have near-identical
scores, and the tie is broken differently between wasm and native. Both
choices are mathematically valid moves under ama's evaluation function.

## What this means for strength

**Native is not weaker.** In tied positions the choice is between near-equally
good moves. Strength equivalence will be confirmed by:

1. Self-play eval (Phase 6 Task 6.3 / `npm run eval -- --a ama-wasm --b ama-native`)
   gating at B/A ratio 0.95–1.05 — the spec's actual strength metric.
2. End-game outcomes in `npm run eval` should match within statistical
   noise.
